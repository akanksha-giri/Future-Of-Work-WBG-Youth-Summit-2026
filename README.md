# Future of Work 2026: Insights from the World Bank Group Youth Summit

## Overview

This project is a research brief based on my participation as a Youth Delegate at the World Bank Group Youth Summit 2026.

Over two days, I attended keynote sessions, panel discussions, regional forums, and the Global Youth Pitch Competition. Rather than treating the summit as a networking event, I approached it as a research opportunity.

This report captures the key ideas, challenges, and solutions discussed throughout the summit, with a particular focus on employment, technology, education, development finance, and youth opportunities in emerging economies.

---

## Why I Created This Report

One statistic stood out to me throughout the summit:

* Approximately 1.2 billion young people are expected to enter the workforce across emerging markets over the next 10–15 years.
* Only around 400 million formal jobs are expected to be created during the same period.

This gap raises important questions about how governments, businesses, educational institutions, and development organizations can prepare for the future of work.

I created this report to organize the discussions, identify recurring themes, and reflect on what these changes may mean for young professionals and communities around the world.

---

## What I Explored

### Workforce Transformation

* The impact of AI and automation on entry-level employment
* The growing gap between education systems and labor market needs
* Long-term consequences of youth unemployment
* Changes in the nature of work across different regions

### Development Finance

* Blended finance approaches
* Income Share Agreements (ISAs)
* Sovereign Skills Bonds
* Alternative data and access to credit

### Regional Perspectives

* Sub-Saharan Africa
* South and East Asia
* Fragile and conflict-affected regions

### Innovation Case Studies

I analyzed three startups presented during the Global Youth Pitch Competition:

* EAGRO
* PANPA Technologies
* Sanka Fresh

For each case, I examined:

* The problem being addressed
* The technology being used
* The business model
* Potential impact and scalability

---

## Key Takeaway

The most important lesson I took away from the summit is that the future of work is not only a technology challenge.

It is also a challenge of access, skills, infrastructure, finance, and implementation.

Many of the most promising solutions were not the most advanced technologies. They were the solutions designed to work within real-world constraints and serve communities that are often overlooked.
